Hormonal Health

PCOS treatment that addresses the root cause

Polycystic ovarian syndrome is more than irregular periods—it's a metabolic and hormonal condition that affects your whole body. We treat the complete picture, not just individual symptoms.

Symptoms We Address

PCOS looks different in everyone

You don't need to have every symptom to have PCOS. You don't even need visible cysts on your ovaries. PCOS is a syndrome—a collection of features that shows up differently in each person.

Common Symptoms We Treat

  • Irregular or absent periods
  • Weight gain, especially around the middle
  • Heavy or prolonged bleeding
  • Skin tags
  • Excess facial or body hair (hirsutism)
  • Dark patches of skin (acanthosis nigricans)
  • Hormonal acne (jawline, chin, back)
  • Fatigue and low energy
  • Hair thinning or loss on scalp
  • Mood swings, anxiety, depression
  • Difficulty losing weight
  • Difficulty getting pregnant

Beyond the basics—testosterone, LH/FSH, AMH. We also run thyroid, lipid panels, vitamin D, fasting insulin. We screen for the complete syndrome, not just individual features.

Based on your symptoms, labs, and goals. Everyone's plan is different. No cookie-cutter protocols. We choose evidence-based medications if needed, supplements and nutrition strategy, movement/exercise, and stress management.

PCOS is a lifelong condition that requires ongoing management. We'll monitor your response, adjust treatment as needed, and support you through different life stages.

Treatment Options

Evidence-based treatment for your PCOS

Treatment depends on your symptoms, your labs, and your goals. We often use a combination approach because PCOS is multifactorial—addressing just one piece rarely solves the puzzle.

Medications

Spironolactone

Anti-androgen that reduces hormonal acne, hirsutism (excess hair growth), and scalp hair thinning. Takes 3–6 months to see full results.

Metformin

Improves insulin sensitivity, supports weight management. First-line for evidence of insulin resistance in PCOS.

Hormonal Birth Control

Regulates periods, reduces androgens, prevents endometrial buildup from anovulatory cycles. Options include pill, patch, IUD, or implant.

Fertility Medications

When you're ready, Letrozole is the first choice for PCOS. Clomid or referral to a fertility specialist when appropriate.

Supplements

Inositol (Myo + D-Chiro)

Evidence-based gold-standard supplement for PCOS. Supports ovulation, may help with weight loss and metabolic parameters.

Vitamin D

Most people with PCOS are deficient. Supports ovarian health and insulin function. We test baseline and reassess.

Magnesium

Supports insulin sensitivity, anxiety, and menstrual cramps. Glycinate form is best tolerated.

Zinc

May help with acne and hair loss. Supports androgen metabolism and skin health.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Anti-inflammatory, supports metabolic health. Quality matters more than dose.

DIM (Diindolylmethane)

Supports healthy estrogen metabolism. May help people with androgen-related symptoms.

Lifestyle Interventions

Nutrition Strategy

Blood sugar stability is the key feature for PCOS. Not about low-carb—it's about balance, protein, healthy fats, and fiber.

Movement That Works

Resistance training helps insulin resistance dramatically. Walking also helps. Builds muscle, increases insulin sensitivity, doesn't spike stress hormones.

Stress Management

Chronic stress worsens insulin, worsens acne, worsens period dysfunction. This is real and addressable.

Sleep Optimization

Poor sleep worsens insulin, hormones, and everything. 7–9 hours is non-negotiable, and we address sleep apnea which is common in PCOS.

Is This Right for You?

This appointment may be right for you if...

  • You've been diagnosed with PCOS but aren't getting comprehensive treatment
  • You suspect you have PCOS but have never been properly evaluated
  • Your periods are irregular, absent, or unpredictable
  • You're struggling with hormonal acne that won't respond to skincare
  • You're dealing with excess hair growth or hair loss
  • You can't lose weight despite doing "everything right"
  • You're planning for pregnancy and want to optimize your fertility
  • You want to understand and address the root causes, not just mask symptoms
