PCOS treatment that addresses the root cause

Polycystic ovarian syndrome is more than irregular periods—it's a metabolic and hormonal condition that affects your whole body. We treat the complete picture, not just individual symptoms.

Symptoms We Care For

PCOS looks different in everyone

You don't need to have every symptom to have PCOS. You don't even need visible cysts on your ovaries. PCOS is a syndrome—a collection of features that shows up differently in each person.

Common PCOS Symptoms

  • Irregular or absent periods
  • Heavy or prolonged bleeding
  • Excess facial or body hair
  • Hormonal acne (jawline, chin, back)
  • Hair thinning or loss on scalp
  • Difficulty losing weight
  • Weight gain (especially around the middle)
  • Skin tags
  • Dark patches of skin
  • Fatigue and low energy
  • Mood swings, anxiety, depression
  • Difficulty getting pregnant

Treatment Options

Evidence-based treatment for your PCOS

Treatment depends on your symptoms, labs, and goals. We often use a combination approach because PCOS is multifactorial—addressing just one piece rarely solves the puzzle.

Medications

Prescription options that target specific PCOS symptoms and underlying mechanisms.

Spironolactone

Anti-androgen that helps with hormonal acne, excess hair growth, and hair loss. Takes 3–6 months to see full results.

Metformin

Improves insulin sensitivity, can help regulate periods and support weight loss. First-line for metabolic PCOS features.

Hormonal Birth Control

Regulates periods, reduces androgens, clears skin, prevents endometrial buildup. Many options available.

Fertility Medications

When you're ready: Letrozole (first-line for PCOS), Clomid, or referral for additional support.

Supplements

Evidence-based supplements that support hormonal and metabolic health in PCOS.

Inositol (Myo + D-Chiro)

Improves insulin sensitivity, supports ovulation, may help with weight and mood. Often called "nature's metformin."

Vitamin D

Most people with PCOS are deficient. Supports insulin sensitivity, mood, and ovarian function. We dose based on your levels.

Magnesium

Supports insulin sensitivity, sleep, anxiety, and menstrual cramps. Glycinate form is best absorbed.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Anti-inflammatory, supports metabolic health and mood. Can help lower androgens.

Lifestyle Interventions

These aren't optional extras—they're foundational to PCOS management and often as effective as medication.

Nutrition Strategy

Blood sugar balance is key: protein with every meal, fiber goal of 25g+/day, limiting refined carbs. Not about restriction—about stabilization.

Movement That Works

Resistance training builds insulin-sensitive muscle. Walking improves insulin sensitivity. Consistency over intensity.

Stress Management

Cortisol worsens insulin resistance and androgen production. Sleep, boundaries, and nervous system regulation matter.

Sleep Optimization

Poor sleep worsens insulin resistance and increases cravings. 7–9 hours, consistent schedule, addressing sleep apnea are priorities.

Is This Right for You?

This may be right for you if...

  • You've been diagnosed with PCOS but aren't getting comprehensive treatment
  • You suspect you have PCOS but have never been properly evaluated
  • Your periods are irregular, absent, or unpredictable
  • You're struggling with hormonal acne that won't respond to skincare
  • You're dealing with excess hair growth or hair loss
  • You can't lose weight despite doing "everything right"
  • You're planning for pregnancy and want to optimize your fertility
  • You want to understand and address the root cause, not just mask symptoms

Insurance & Cost

Medication costs vary. Metformin and spironolactone are inexpensive generics ($4–15/month). Birth control coverage depends on your plan.
