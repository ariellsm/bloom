The Problem

Why "normal" labs don't tell the whole story

Standard lab panels check your blood sugar and cholesterol—but by the time those are flagged as "abnormal," metabolic dysfunction has often been brewing for years. We test earlier markers that catch problems when they're still reversible.

What Standard Labs Miss

  • Inflammatory markers (hs-CRP, homocysteine)
  • Lipoprotein particle size and number
  • Fasting Insulin (not just glucose)
  • HOMA-IR (insulin resistance calculation)
  • Vitamin D levels
  • Thyroid (full panel, not just TSH)
  • Uric acid (metabolic and heart risk)
  • Ferritin (iron storage)
What We Test

Comprehensive metabolic assessment

Insulin & Blood Sugar

Insulin resistance is often the first domino to fall in metabolic disease—and it's invisible on standard panels.

Fasting Insulin

Elevated insulin is often the first sign of metabolic dysfunction—years before blood sugar goes up. This is the test that catches problems early.

HOMA-IR

Uses insulin and glucose to calculate insulin resistance. A key marker most providers don't calculate but we consider essential.

HbA1c

3-month average of blood sugar. Useful for seeing the bigger picture beyond a single fasting reading.

Fasting Glucose

Standard but important. We look at the trend over time, not just whether it's technically "normal."

Cardiovascular Risk

Heart disease risk is about more than cholesterol numbers—we look at the full picture.

Full Lipid Panel

Total cholesterol, LDL, HDL, triglycerides. The nuance matters more than the numbers—we look at the patterns and trends.

Triglyceride/HDL Ratio

One of the best predictors of insulin resistance and cardiovascular outcomes. Ideally under 2.

hs-CRP

High-sensitivity C-reactive protein measures inflammation—an independent risk factor for heart disease that standard panels skip.

Lp(a) and ApoB

Advanced markers that better predict cardiovascular risk than standard cholesterol, especially with a family history.

Supporting Markers

Thyroid, nutrients, and other factors that directly affect energy, metabolism, and how you feel day to day.

Complete Thyroid Panel

TSH, Free T4, Free T3, thyroid antibodies. Your thyroid affects metabolism, energy, weight, and mood. We test it all.

Ferritin

Iron storage. Low levels cause fatigue, brain fog, and contribute to metabolic issues. Often missed on standard panels.

Vitamin D

Affects insulin sensitivity, immune function, and mood. Most people are deficient and don't know it.

Uric Acid

Not just for gout—elevated uric acid is tied to insulin resistance, fatty liver, and cardiovascular risk.

Is This Right for You?

This assessment may be right for you if...

  • Your labs are "normal" but you don't feel well
  • You've been fatigued, foggy, or can't lose weight despite trying
  • You have a family history of diabetes or heart disease
  • You have PCOS or suspect insulin resistance
  • You're in perimenopause or menopause and noticing metabolic changes
  • You want to approach your health proactively, not just wait for problems
  • You crave sugar, crash after meals, or feel "hangry" frequently
  • You've been told your cholesterol is "borderline" but given no plan
What Happens Next

From testing to action plan

1

Initial Consultation

We'll discuss your symptoms, health history, and goals. We order comprehensive labs tailored to what you need—not one-size-fits-all basic panels.

2

Lab Review Visit

We go through results together—not just the flagged values, but the patterns and trends. You'll leave understanding what your numbers actually mean.

3

Personalized Plan

Based on your results: nutrition strategies, targeted supplements if needed, movement recommendations, medication if appropriate. Specific, actionable steps.

4

Follow-Up & Monitoring

We recheck labs in 3–6 months to track progress. Metabolic health is modifiable—we want to see your numbers improve.
